Rules of the road
Steering and Sailing Rules - Section Three

Rule Nineteen Conduct of Vessels in Restricted Visibility.

This Rule applies to vessels out of sight of one another when navigating in conditions of restricted ability. Restrictions are laid down on speed and conditions of engines action to be taken after election by radar and methods to determine risk of collision.

A. All vessels operating in or near conditions of restricted visibility are covered by this Rule. Such a vessel must also give the appropriate sound signals as prescribed in Rule 35. Vessels not in sight of one another should not give the warning signals prescribed in Rule 34.
