Rules of the road
Steering and Sailing Rules - Section Three

Rule Nineteen Conduct of Vessels in Restricted Visibility (contd.)

C. All vessels must take into account the conditions of restricted visibility when complying with Section I of Part B.

Captains and navigating officers must apply Rules and dealing with lookouts risk of collision and avoiding action.

Rules relating to navigation in narrow channels and in traffic separation schemes also apply in conditions of restricted visibility.

A responsible lookout should be posted in restricted visibility and have continuous watch on the radar at all times by a competent person.

More substantial alterations of course are required so that your actions are observed and understood by other vessels in restricted visibility, or out of sight.
