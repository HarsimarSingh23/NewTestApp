Rules of the road
Steering and Sailing Rules - Section Three

Rule Nineteen Conduct of Vessels in Restricted Visibility (contd.)

Take all the way off if necessary

Courts have held that that vessels navigating without radar should have reversed their engines after hearing a fog signal forward of the beam in the following instances:

● When the signal was first heard in close proximity.

● Where the signal was heard dead ahead.

● Where the signals were narrowing on the bow.

● Where a vessel was seen to loom out of the fog but her course was not immediately apparent.

● Where a sailing vessels fog signal was heard forward of the beam and.

● Where the signal was that of a vessel at anchor and the tide was setting towards her.
