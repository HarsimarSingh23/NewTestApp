Basic Principles for Safe Watchkeeping

International Convention on Standards of Training Certification and watchkeeping for Seafarers ANNEX

Regulation VIII/2 - Watchkeeping Arrangements and Principles 

1. A safe and continuous watch appropriate to the prevailing circumstances and conditions should be maintained on all seagoing vessels at all times 

2. Every ships captain should ensure that watchkeeping arrangements are adequate for maintaining a.
Safe watch taking into account the prevailing circumstances and conditions and under his general direction:

	A. Officers in charge of the navigation watch are responsible for navigating the ship safely 	during their watch and that they shall be physically present on the bridge or in a place close 	by ( like the chart room or the bridge control room ).
