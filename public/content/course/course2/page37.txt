49. Despite the duties and obligations of pilots, their presence on board does not relieve the <bi> Master <.bi> or officer in charge of the watch from their duties and obligations for the safety of the ship. The <bi> Master <.bi> and the pilot shall exchange information regarding <bi> navigation <.bi> procedures, local conditions, and the ship's characteristics. The <bi> Master <.bi> and/or the officer in charge of the navigational watch shall cooperate closely with the pilot and maintain an accurate check on the ship's position and movement.

50. If in any doubt as to the pilot's actions or intentions, the officer in charge of the navigational watch shall seek clarification from the pilot and, if doubt still exists, shall notify the <bi> Master <.bi> immediately and take whatever action is necessary before the <bi> Master <.bi> arrives.

Navigation with Pilot on board

49. The Captain and the Officer of the Watch are still responsible for the safety of their vessel inspire of having a harbor Pilot on board. They shall cooperate closely with the Pilot to ensure the set course and speed is maintained.

50. The Officer of the Watch shall clarify any doubts with the Pilot and if one should still exist notify the Captain immediately.
