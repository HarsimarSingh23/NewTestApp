Steering and Sailing Rules - Section One

Rule Five Lookouts and Watchkeeping (contd.)

Lookout (contd.)

The Lookout Man

On all but the smallest vessels a seaman should be posted on look-out duty from dusk to dawn and by day when visibility is restricted. A proper lookout is necessary for safe watchkeeping.

A good lookout is usually posted forward on the forecastle unless weather conditions prohibit it with constant communication to the bridge. This is done to prevent his attention being distracted by.
Conversation and activities taking place on the bridge.
