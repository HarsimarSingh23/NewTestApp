9. Look-out
In addition to maintaining a proper <bi> look-out <.bi> for the purpose of fully appraising the situation and the risk of collision, stranding and other dangers to navigation, the duties of the <bi> look-out <.bi> shall include the detection of ships or aircraft in distress, shipwrecked persons, wrecks and debris. In maintaining a <bi> look-out <.bi> the following shall be observed:

(a) the <bi> look-out <.bi> must be able to give full attention to the keeping of a proper <bi> look-out <.bi> and no other duties shall be undertaken or assigned which could interfere with that task;

(b) the duties of the <bi> look-out <.bi> and <bi> helmsman <.bi> are separate and the <bi> helmsman <.bi> shall not be considered to be the <bi> look-out <.bi> while steering, except in small ships where an unobstructed all-round view is provided at the steering position and there is no impairment of night vision or other impediment to the keeping of a proper look-out. The officer in charge of the watch may be the sole <bi> look-out <.bi> in daylight provided that on each such occasion:

(i) the situation has been carefully assessed and it has been established without doubt that it is safe to do so;

(ii) full account has been taken of all relevant factors including, but not limited to:
- state of weather
- visibility
- traffic density
- proximity of danger to navigation
- the attention necessary when navigating in or near traffic separation schemes;

(iii) assistance is immediately available to be summoned to the <bi> bridge <.bi> when any change in the situation so requires.

9. Look-out

A proper lookout shall 

● Keep watch for collision stranding and other dangers to navigation.

● detect other ships or aircraft in distress and pass on this information to the Officer of the Watch or the Captain.

● keep watch for shipwrecked persons wrecks and floating or other debris.

a. Lookout personnel should not be assigned any other task that will affect their duties.
