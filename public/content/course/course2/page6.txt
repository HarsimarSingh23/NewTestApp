Steering and Sailing Rules - Section One

Rule Five Lookouts and Watchkeeping (contd.)

Lookout (contd.)

To use All Available Means Appropriate.


The lookout posted should make proper use of all available instruments and equipment in addition to both senses of sight and hearing. This means that a look-out should have recourse to binoculars, radar VHF radio and whatever other electronic means the vessel is equipped with to report another object which could in future cause a collision.


Radar is an essential part of the lookouts aid in cases of.
Restricted visibility.
