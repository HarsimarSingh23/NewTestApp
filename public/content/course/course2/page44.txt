Steering and Sailing Rules - Section One

Rule Six Safe Speed (contd.)

● Safe speed is one in which a vessel will be able to stop within half the range of visibility. However each vessels safe speed should be judged on a casetocase basis. Attempts have been made to quantify speed in relation to the size of the vessel and the visibility levels but no final decision has been reached even at various limo meetings.

● Safe speed depends on the vessels length turning circle drought and type of machinery. Most of this information is now supplied to navigating officers who are expected to be familiar with the navigating.
Characteristics of their own ship.
