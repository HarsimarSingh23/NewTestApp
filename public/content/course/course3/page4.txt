Rule Seven Risk of Collision (contd.)


>>> Click to see rule

a. Every vessel shall use all available means appropriate to the prevailing circumstances and conditions to determine if risk of a collision exists.  If there is any doubt, such risk shall be deemed to exist.

b. Proper use shall be made of <bi> radar <.bi> equipment if fitted and operational, including long-range scanning to obtain early warning of risk of collision and <bi> radar <.bi> plotting or equivalent systematic observation of detected objects.

c. Assumptions shall not be made on the basis of <bi> scanty <.bi> information, especially <bi> scanty <.bi> <bi> radar <.bi> information.

d. In determining if risk of collision exists, the following considerations shall be among those taken into account :

i. Such risk shall be deemed to exist if the compass bearing of an approaching vessel does not apreciably change.

ii. Such risk may sometimes exist even when an appreciable bearing change is evident, particularly when appproaching a very large vessel or a tow or when approaching a vessel at close range.

d. The following could mean the risk of collision is high and necessary action should be taken to rectify matters.

	I. The compass bearing of an approaching vessel remains constant.
	II. If the vessel is extra-large a changing compass bearing may still not <bi> rule <.bi> out the risk of collision.
