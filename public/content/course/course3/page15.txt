Rules of the road
Steering and Sailing Rules - Section One

Rule Eight: Action to Avoid Collision

B. Alterations that are as large as possible should be made to course speed or heading unless space depth or surrounding traffic prevent such alterations being made. This enables the other vessel to take adequate note of the alteration and make arrangements of her own.

In restricted visibility alterations of course and speed should be substantial so as to be readily apparent to another vessel observing her by radar. A alteration is sufficient for course and should preferably in the order of or alterations of speed take longer to implement and are less likely to be observed. A STOP order for engines should be given followed by a SLOW AHEAD or DEAD SLOW, later.
