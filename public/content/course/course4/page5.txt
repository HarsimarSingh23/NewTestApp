a. A vessel proceeding along the course of a narrow channel or <bi> fairway <.bi> shall keep as near to the outer limit of the channel or <bi> fairway <.bi> which lies to her starboard side as is safe and practicable.

b. A vessel of less than 20 mtrs. in length or a sailing vessel shall not <bi> impede <.bi> the <bi> passage <.bi> of a vessel which can safely navigate only within a narrow channel or fairway.

c. A vessel engaged in fishing shall not <bi> impede <.bi> the <bi> passage <.bi> of any other vessel navigating within a narrow channel or fairway.

d. A vessel shall not cross a narrow channel or <bi> fairway <.bi> if such crossing impedes the <bi> passage <.bi> of a vessel which can safely navigate only within such a channel or fairway. The latter vessel may use the sound signal prescribed in <bi> Rule <.bi> 34(d) if in doubt as to the intention of the crossing vessel.

(ii) This <bi> Rule <.bi> does not relieve the overtaking vessel of any obligation under <bi> Rule <.bi> 13.

f. A vessel nearing a bend or an area of a narrow channel or <bi> fairway <.bi> where other vessels may be obscured by an intervening obstruction shall navigate with particular alertness and caution and shall sound the appropriate signal prescribed in <bi> Rule <.bi> 34(e).

g. Any vessel shall, if the circumstances of the case admit, avoid anchoring in a narrow channel.
