Rule Ten Traffic Separation Schemes (contd.)

Routeing Systems and their Use (contd.)

	● Deepwater routes should be left to those vessels that require the exclusive use of this channel owing to their drought.

	● Precautionary areas should be avoided by passing ships not making use of the traffic separation scheme deepwater route or entering or leaving adjacent ports.

	● In today routes ships should as far as practicable keep to the starboard side.

	● Arrows printed on charts in connection with routeing systems indicate the general direction of traffic
flow ships need not be strict about these when setting their courses.

	● The signal YG means You appear not to be complying with the traffic separation scheme and is provided in the international code of signals for appropriate use
