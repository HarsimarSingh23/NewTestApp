RULES OF THE ROAD
Sound and Light Signal

Rule Thirty Seven: Distress Signals (contd.)

When a vessel is in distress and requires assistance, she shall use or exhibit one or more of the following distress signals :

Annexure IV (contd.)

k. slowly and repeatedly raising and lowering arms outstretched to each side;

l. the radiotelegraph alarm signal;

m. the radiotelephone alarm signal;

n. signals transmitted by emergency position-indicating radio beacons;

o. approved signals transmitted by radiocommunication systems, including survival craft <bi> radar <.bi> transponders.
