Rule Twenty Four Towing and Pushing (contd.)

These paragraphs apply to warships since they are not designed to carry out extended towing operations.

In cases where assistance is to be rendered to vessels in distress all available measures must be taken to warn other waterway users of the presence of the distressed vessel or object being towed.

(h) In case it is not practical to display the lights and shapes prescribed in paras (e) or (g) adequate steps must be taken to light the vessel or object being towed.

(i) If it is not practical to display the lights prescribed in paras a or c of this Rule the towing vessel.
