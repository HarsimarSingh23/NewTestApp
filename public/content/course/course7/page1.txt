Rules of the road
Lights and Shape

Rule Twenty: Application

This section comprises Rules relating to Lights and Shapes to be displayed.

Lights are to be displayed

● In all weathers from sunset to sunrise

● During daylight in restricted visibility

● In other circumstances when deemed necessary

No other lights that could confuse a lookout on another vessel should be employed on your ship. If any lights are lost they should be replaced as soon as possible from ships stores oil or battery-operated lanterns should be properly maintained and kept ready for use.

Any delay in repairing or replacing lost lights due to bad weather should be noted in the ship's log.

Deck lighting may be switched on in port or while at anchor. These working lights should be switched off when anchor is weighed or when the ship is underway.

In daylight in clear visibility, dayshapes are to be exhibited.
